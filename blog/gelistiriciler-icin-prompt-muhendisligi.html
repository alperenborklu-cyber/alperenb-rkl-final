Geliştiriciler İçin Prompt Mühendisliği

Yazar Alperen Börklü 5 Dk Okuma

Prompt engineering is the art of communicating effectively with AI — and it has become one of the most valuable skills a developer can have. As AI tools take on larger roles in the software development process, the quality of what you ask for determines the quality of what you get. A vague prompt produces vague code. A precise, well-structured prompt produces precise, well-structured results.

Why It Matters for Coding

Most developers underestimate how much their prompts shape AI output. Saying "write a function to process payments" is vastly less effective than specifying the language, the payment provider, the expected inputs and outputs, error handling requirements, and any relevant architectural constraints. Context is everything — the more you provide, the less the AI has to guess.

Key Techniques That Work

Role prompting — telling the AI "You are a senior TypeScript engineer" — shapes the tone and depth of its responses. Chain-of-thought prompting — asking the AI to "think step by step before writing code" — dramatically improves accuracy on complex logic. Few-shot examples — providing one or two examples of the input/output pattern you want — anchor the AI's output to your exact format and expectations.

Iterating Like a Pro

The best prompt engineers don't expect perfection on the first attempt. They treat prompting as a dialogue: review the output, identify where the AI went wrong, and refine the prompt to eliminate the ambiguity that caused the error. Over time, this builds a library of effective prompt patterns tailored to your specific projects and workflows — a genuine professional asset.
